Impact of Terrorism on World Development

What is this app about?

Definition of terrorism
"The threatened or actual use of illegal force and violence by a non-state actor to attain a political, economic, religious, or social goal through fear, coercion, or intimidation."

The idea of the project is to give insightful information about the potential correlation that exists between the development of a country and terrorist attacks occuring in that particular country. To do so, we use the dataset provided by the World Bank along with an open-source global terrorism database.

The former provide information about world development indicators such as CO2 emissions, birth death crude, number of hospital beds, all of this for more than 180 countries from 1960 to 2015. The latter dataset provide information on location, tactics, perpetrators, targets, and outcomes for more than 170'000 terrorist attacks worldwide, from 1970 to 2016.

How to use it?

  1. Close this menu.
  2. Click on the country you are interested in.
  3. The points represent terrorist attacks that happend between 1970 and 2016.
  4. Click on the bottom right button to see details and explore correlations with social and economic indicators.

Data Story

The main idea was to make an appropriate selection of indicators from both databases to be able to show if any correlation could be drawn from it. The difficulty was to choose the most meaningful and straightforward indicators. After a long and meticulous inspection, we selected at first the following indicators, yearly, for each country:

Global Terrorism Database
  • Total number of attacks.
  • Total number of victims.

In front of the compeling evidence that using only these two indicators was not enough to draw clear correlations between them and development indicators, we had to find a solution or potential improvement. Thus, we started to look for an index which encompasses for more variables and found the " Global Terrorism Index", or GTI, which is common in official reports done by institutional entities. This index is based on multiple variables to determine each country's score:

  • Total number of terrorist attacks.
  • Total number of fatalities caused by terrorism.
  • Total number of injuries caused by terrorism.
  • Approximate level of total property damage from terrorist attacks.

The weightings used to aggregate these variables into a single score were determined by consultation with the GPI Expert Panel:

Variable Weight
Total number of terrorist attacks 1
Total number of fatalities caused by terrorism 3
Total number of injuries caused by terrorism 0.5
Sum of property damages measure 2

Where the latest score "Sum of property damages measure" is further splitted into 4 different levels, each with its corresponding score:

Damage level Score
Unknown 0
Minor (likely < $1 million) 1
Major (likely between $1 million and $1 billion) 2
Catastrophic (likely > $1 billion) 3

The GTI is then calculated by a weighted sum of all the aforementioned indicators and we obtain this second version of the indicators' list, allowing us to rapidly grasp the context in which a country is:

  • Number of attacks per year.
  • Number of victims per year.
  • Global Terrorism Index
World Development Indicators
  • Economic
    • Tourism - number of arrivals
    • Foreign direct investment (net inflows % GDP)
    • Gross Domestic Product (annual % growth)
    • Good and service imports (annual % growth)
    • Good and service exports (annual % growth)
  • Social & Health
    • Birth rate (per 1,000 people)
    • Death rate (per 1,000 people)
    • Armed forces personnel, total

These indicators help us to understand the development process of a specific country in the following sectors : economy, social health, population and wealth. The delicate part was then to see if a link can exist between a pair of indicators chosen from each one of these two databases. However, this way of proceeding has a severe weak point: comparing only two indicators is equivalent to reducing a causal link to an unique variable while it includes several. To overcome on part of this issue, we decided to aggregate a subset of the GTD indicators in order to use the Global Terrorism Index (GTI) scoring system. This score is used in the well-known report of the same name published annually by Institute for Economics and Peace (IEP) to account for the relative impact of terrorism for a given year.
